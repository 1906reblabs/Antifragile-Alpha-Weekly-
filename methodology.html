The Framework

How We Think About Risk & Return

Every stock in Antifragile Alpha Weekly is selected through a disciplined, repeatable process — macro regime alignment, fundamental stress-testing, and asymmetric position sizing. This page documents that process in full.

1. Philosophy 2. Barbell Construction 3. Screening Criteria 4. Position Sizing 5. Stop-Loss Discipline 6. Weekly Process 7. Rules Engine 8. Limitations
§ 01

Core Philosophy

Antifragile Alpha Weekly is built around a single conviction: the JSE systematically misprices asymmetry. Most institutional research on South African equities is either consensus-anchored, backward-looking, or constrained by AUM size to only cover large-caps. That creates persistent inefficiencies in mid-cap commodity producers, domestically focused financials, and smaller growth compounders.

Our framework is not momentum-chasing, nor contrarian for its own sake. It is regime-aware: we ask what macroeconomic and geopolitical environment we are actually in, identify which business models benefit most from that regime, then buy the highest-operating-leverage expression of that thesis at a price that gives us asymmetric upside relative to a defined loss scenario.

The term antifragile — borrowed from Nassim Taleb — does not mean immune to volatility. It means structured to benefit from it. A portfolio with well-sized positions in businesses with convex payoff profiles, combined with disciplined stop losses, gains from volatility in aggregate even when individual positions fail.

Core principle: We are not trying to be right more often than we are wrong. We are trying to ensure that when we are right, we profit significantly more than we lose when we are wrong.

§ 02

Barbell Portfolio Construction

Every edition maintains a three-tier barbell portfolio across approximately 10–12 JSE positions. The tiers are not arbitrary buckets — they reflect different reward/risk profiles, time horizons, and thesis types.

~55%
Safe Core
High-conviction, quality businesses at fair prices. Lower upside, lower drawdown risk. Anchors the portfolio.

~39%
High Upside
Asymmetric opportunities: deep value, macro-driven, or catalyst-led. 2×–4× return potential with thesis-defined downside.

◆ Min position: 5%
◆ Cash buffer: ~1% for rebalancing
◆ Max simultaneous moonshots: 2

Tier Promotion & Demotion

Positions are not permanently classified. A High Upside stock can be promoted to Safe Core once the original thesis is confirmed and the position has appreciated 30%+, stop has been raised, and remaining upside still justifies the allocation. A Safe Core stock that shows deteriorating fundamentals, missed guidance, or a broken macro thesis is demoted or exited — not held out of inertia.

Moonshot positions are by definition speculative. They are sized at 5–6% and not added to if they rise without fundamental confirmation. They may be exited at a total loss if the thesis timeline expires (typically 18–36 months) without a catalyst.

§ 03

Screening Criteria

Stocks are sourced from the JSE Main Board, A2X, and AltX. The initial screen runs on two levels: a quantitative gate to eliminate structurally weak businesses, and a qualitative filter to surface genuine asymmetry.

Quantitative Gate

📊

Revenue Trend

Minimum 2 consecutive years of revenue growth, or a documented single-year dip with a credible, data-backed recovery narrative.

💰

Balance Sheet Solvency

Net debt / EBITDA below 3× for industrials and financials. For miners: preference for net-cash or low-leverage positions — debt amplifies downside in commodity cycles.

📈

Operating Leverage

In commodity or rate-sensitive theses, we require AISC or cost structure data to calculate revenue sensitivity at multiple price scenarios.

🔁

Return on Equity

ROE above 15% for financial sector compounders. For cyclicals and commodity plays, ROE is assessed at mid-cycle prices, not at the current peak.

💵

Free Cash Flow

FCF yield above 3% for Safe Core holdings. High Upside and Moonshot positions may carry negative FCF if the business is pre-inflection or capex-heavy ahead of a step-change in output.

📉

Liquidity Minimum

Minimum average daily traded value of R500k for High Upside and Safe Core tiers. Moonshots may have lower liquidity with explicit liquidity risk disclosure.

Qualitative Filter

Stocks that clear the quantitative gate are then assessed for three qualitative dimensions:

  • ◆ Macro Regime Alignment. Does the current or imminent macro regime (gold price direction, SARB rate trajectory, ZAR trend, China demand cycle, global risk appetite) structurally support this business's earnings growth? We do not fight the macro — we find the businesses best positioned to benefit from it.
  • ◆ Catalyst Visibility. Is there a near-term, identifiable catalyst — results announcement, production update, policy decision, earnings upgrade cycle — that could close the gap between current price and intrinsic value within 12 months?
  • ◆ Management Track Record. We heavily discount managements that have a history of capital destruction, empire-building acquisitions at peak multiples, or persistent guidance misses without credible corrective action.

Current macro regime (Ed. 05, Apr 2026): Gold recovery confirmed after margin-call flush. ZAR strengthening from R19.2 shock. SARB 2-cut path credible post oil decline. Overweight: ZAR gold miners, big-4 banks, Naspers/Prosus digital assets. Underweight: rand-cost oil-sensitive businesses, high-PE offshore earners.

§ 04

Position Sizing

Position sizes are determined by three inputs: conviction level, thesis clarity, and asymmetry ratio. We do not size equally. Equal weighting ignores that some theses are far more legible — and far more asymmetric — than others.

Tier Typical Size Max Size Rationale
Safe Core — Anchor 12–14% 14% Highest conviction, confirmed macro tailwind, strong FCF
Safe Core — Standard 9–11% 11% High conviction, catalyst visible within 6 months
High Upside — Core 9–11% 11% Clear asymmetry, binary catalyst or cycle play
High Upside — Satellite 6–8% 8% Solid thesis, less catalyst clarity or earlier stage
Moonshot 5–6% 6% Speculative; sized to limit total bleed if thesis fails

Sizing on Adds and Reduces

We do not average down on losing positions as a default behaviour. If a position moves against us, we first reassess whether the thesis is intact. If the macro has structurally changed against the thesis, we exit. If it is a short-term price dislocation on an intact thesis, we may add — but only within the tier's maximum allocation, and only with a revised stop.

We do add to winning positions when a new catalyst emerges that was not in the original thesis, up to the tier maximum. We never let a single position exceed 14% regardless of how well it is performing — concentration risk in a single name is incompatible with the antifragile framework.

§ 05

Stop-Loss Discipline

Every published position carries an explicit stop-loss price. Stops are not suggestions — they are pre-defined exit triggers that are assessed at each weekly edition. Stops serve two purposes: capital protection on new entries, and profit protection on appreciated positions.

Stop Setting on Entry

Initial stops are set at a technical or fundamental level that invalidates the thesis. For mining stocks this is typically below a key support level and below AISC breakeven at the relevant commodity price. For financials it is typically below the most recent earnings trough or below the 52-week low in a confirmed uptrend.

Rule: The stop-loss price is published before the position is entered. It is not moved downward after entry under any circumstances. It may only be raised as the position appreciates.

Stop Ratcheting (Trailing Logic)

Once a position has appreciated by more than 30% from entry, we evaluate whether to raise the stop to protect at least 50% of the unrealised gain. This is the STOP_RAISE_ON_THESIS_CONFIRMATION rule encoded in the rules engine. The mechanics are:

Gain from EntryStop ActionLogic
+0% to +20%Hold original stopThesis unconfirmed; original risk still valid
+20% to +50%Evaluate raiseRaise if thesis confirmed AND macro intact
+50%+Raise to protect 50%+ of gainsProfit protection mode; let remainder run
+100%+Raise aggressivelyRe-assess tier classification; consider reduce

Thesis-Invalidation Stops

Price action alone is not always the right stop trigger for longer-duration theses. For positions where the core driver is a macro variable (e.g. ZAR gold price for miners), we also define thesis-invalidation conditions — specific macro outcomes that would render the position untenable regardless of where the price is. These are disclosed in each edition's position commentary.

§ 06

Weekly Research Process

Each edition follows a structured weekly workflow. The sequence matters: macro-first, then sector, then stock — never the reverse. Conviction in a stock without macro alignment is just noise.

01
Macro Regime Review

Assess the current week's key macro inputs: spot gold (USD/oz and ZAR/oz), ZAR/USD, Brent crude, ALSI 4-week and 12-month performance, and any SARB guidance or global central bank signals. Update the regime classification (risk-on / risk-off / transitional).

02
Catalyst Calendar Scan

Identify all results announcements, production updates, dividend declarations, SENS filings, and macro events due in the coming 1–4 weeks that affect current or watchlist holdings.

03
Portfolio Position Review

Review each active position against its published buy zone, current price, stop, and target. Assess whether thesis is intact, partially confirmed, or broken. Flag any stops that need ratcheting upward.

04
Watchlist Screening

Run the watchlist through updated macro and fundamental filters. Any watchlist stock whose entry conditions have been met in the current week is a candidate for portfolio inclusion. Apply the quantitative gate and qualitative filter.

05
Scorecard & Rules Engine Update

Update the portfolio scorecard — tracking entry price, current price, and % gain/loss for each position. Run the active rules engine checks to identify any regime-shift signals, forced exit conditions, or new entry triggers.

06
Edition Publication

Compile the weekly edition with updated top-10 screen, macro analysis, 3 hidden-truth insights, and 1 featured deep-dive. Publish the paywall version publicly and the unlocked version to subscribers. Update the portfolio scorecard, stops, and targets for any changes made this week.

§ 07

The Rules Engine

The rules engine is a living set of named, codified decision rules that are derived from past thesis outcomes. Every rule is triggered by a specific, measurable condition and produces a specific portfolio action. Rules are added after an event confirms or refutes a hypothesis — never before. They are documented in each edition's internal report and carried forward permanently.

As of Edition 05 (April 18, 2026), the active rules are:

R-01

GOLD_RECOVERY_VELOCITY

When gold drops more than 15% in fewer than 5 trading days due to apparent forced selling or margin-call dynamics (evidenced by correlation breakdown with USD), treat the dip as a buy signal for net-debt-free ZAR gold miners. Apply only when ZAR gold (R/oz) has not broken its 12-month uptrend. Derived from: Apr 9–10, 2026 gold flush and subsequent recovery to $4,867.

R-02

CEASEFIRE_OIL_SARB_CASCADE

A confirmed ceasefire in a major oil-producing or transit region that produces a sustained 8%+ decline in Brent crude increases the probability of an additional SARB rate cut within 2 meetings. On this signal: overweight big-4 banks (rate-sensitive NII), reduce or exit rand-cost oil-sensitive industrials. Derived from: Israel-Lebanon ceasefire Apr 17, 2026; Brent -10% to $71.

R-03

PRE_RESULTS_DO_NOT_REDUCE

Within 10 calendar days of a results announcement where the company has guided HEPS growth of 15%+ (or where consensus expects this), do not reduce a profitable position regardless of near-term price action. The information asymmetry of a likely beat is greater than the cost of short-term volatility. Applicable to: CPI (Capitec) pre-April 22, 2026 results.

R-04

STOP_RAISE_ON_THESIS_CONFIRMATION

When the original thesis for a position is explicitly confirmed (e.g. a production milestone hit, an earnings beat above the guided range, a macro variable crossing the predicted level), raise the stop-loss to protect at least 50% of unrealised profits. Do not wait for the position to give back 25% before acting. Applied to: PAN (stop raised from R50 to R68 after +106% gain).

R-05

HIGHEST_OPERATING_LEVERAGE_ENTRY

In commodity super-cycles, when selecting between multiple miners exposed to the same commodity, prioritise the producer with the highest revenue-to-AISC ratio at current spot prices. A lower-cost producer with the same commodity exposure produces exponentially more free cash flow per unit of commodity price increase. Framework used for: PAN selection over HAR at Edition 01 entry; HAR added at Edition 05 as leverage diversifier.

New rules are added as new thesis outcomes accumulate. The full rules engine history is documented in each edition's unlocked subscriber report.

§ 08

Known Limitations

No methodology is perfect. We document our known limitations not as disclaimers but as active areas of improvement.

⚠️

Survivorship in Rules

Rules are derived from confirmed events. They may overfit to specific market conditions and fail under different regimes. We test each rule before adding it and retire rules that produce false positives across 3+ events.

🌍

JSE Liquidity Risk

Several positions — particularly AltX and smaller A2X names — carry meaningful bid-ask spread and low daily volume. Theoretical entry zones may be wider in practice. Moonshot positions are particularly exposed to this.

🔮

Macro Timing Risk

Regime identification is probabilistic. We may correctly identify the direction of a macro trend but be early by 3–9 months. This is managed by sizing into positions across the stated buy zone rather than all-in on a single day.

📰

Information Lag

This is an independent research publication. We do not have access to management meetings, sell-side model databases, or real-time SENS processing systems. Our research is based on public information only.
