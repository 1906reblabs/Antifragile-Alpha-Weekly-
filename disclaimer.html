Investment Risk Disclosure

Investing in equities, securities, and financial instruments involves significant risk, including the risk of total loss of capital. Past performance — including any picks referenced in Antifragile Alpha reports or performance tracking tables — is not indicative of future results.

Specific risks you should be aware of before acting on any information published by Antifragile Alpha include:

  • Market risk: Share prices can decline significantly and rapidly due to market conditions, sentiment, or macroeconomic events entirely beyond any analyst's control or prediction.
  • Liquidity risk: Certain stocks covered, particularly on AltX, CTSE, or 4AX, may have low trading volumes and wide bid-ask spreads, making entry and exit difficult or costly at published price levels.
  • Concentration risk: Reports cover a focused list of equities. A concentrated portfolio based on this research carries higher volatility than a diversified portfolio.
  • Currency risk: ZAR-denominated investments are subject to rand depreciation risk when measured in foreign currency terms, and vice versa.
  • Commodity risk: Many picks involve commodity-exposed businesses (gold, platinum, iron ore, oil). Commodity prices are volatile and subject to geopolitical and macroeconomic forces outside local control.
  • Small and mid-cap risk: Smaller companies referenced may have less analyst coverage, higher volatility, less liquid markets, and greater operational risk than large-cap names.
  • Leverage risk: Any use of Single Stock Futures (SSFs), Contracts for Difference (CFDs), options, or other leveraged instruments multiplies both potential gains and potential losses and may result in losses exceeding your initial capital.
  • Regime change risk: Macro regime changes — as documented in Antifragile Alpha reports — can render a thesis invalid rapidly. The publication makes no guarantee of thesis validity between weekly editions.
