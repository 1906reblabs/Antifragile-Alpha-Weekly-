SA Equities (Apr 9, 2026)

📅 April 9, 2026 📊 Data: Market close Apr 8, 2026 🏦 JSE · A2X · AltX · CTSE · 4AX

This is a regime-change edition. Since our April 1 report, SA's macro landscape has shifted materially: gold has surged past $4,700/oz, the SARB now projects only 1 rate cut in 2026 (revised from 2), the rand has weakened ~6% on oil-driven inflation fears, and Sasol has become the JSE's most explosive large-cap. The Middle East conflict (Iran war, Strait of Hormuz disruptions) has injected a major supply shock into global oil markets. SA, as a net oil importer, faces elevated fuel prices that are feeding into CPI forecasts. The SARB's March 26 hold cited this explicitly, and its new base case projects CPI rising to ~4% in Q2 2026 before easing. This pushes the first meaningful rate cut to H2 at best.

However, the same conflict has sent gold above $4,700/oz — a staggering move that dramatically reprices SA gold miners. Pan African Resources (PAN), DRD Gold, Harmony, and Sibanye are now operating with extraordinary free cash flow margins. This is the single biggest alpha-generating event for SA equities in years.

Meanwhile, Sasol (SOL) — previously underappreciated — has surged over 200% YoY and ~50% in just 4 weeks. The coal-to-liquids business benefits directly from higher oil prices, and the market has repriced it aggressively. Some of this move is now priced in, but the question is what comes next.

"The antifragile investor doesn't panic at regime changes — they re-screen fast, rotate deliberately, and find the new asymmetries before the crowd catches up."

Key implication for the barbell: We rotate the defensive core to include gold miners as a partial inflation hedge. We trim pure rate-cycle plays (banks) slightly given reduced cut probability. We add energy exposure via Sasol (with appropriate stop-loss given the run). The moonshot rotates to a platinum/PGM play given the metals complex re-rating.

Regime-Aware Screening Methodology ✓ Free Preview

Our multi-factor model is unchanged, but regime overlays have been updated to reflect the new macro environment. We now run two passes: the standard factor screen, then a regime filter that reweights for the current commodity super-cycle and inflation shock.

  • Gold Overlay (new weight: HIGH): At $4,700/oz, any SA gold producer with debt below 1× EBITDA and growing output is aggressively re-rated. We screen for: ZAR gold price per oz vs AISC, net debt, output growth, dividend policy. PAN, DRD qualify clearly. Sibanye qualifies on a risk-adjusted basis.
  • Oil/Energy Pass-Through: SA is a net oil importer. We now PENALISE companies with high fuel exposure in their COGS (airlines, logistics, plastics). We FAVOUR companies that benefit: Sasol (producer), mining (rand-denominated revenue on falling rand).
  • Rate Path Revision: Reduced SARB cut probability means banks lose some multiple-expansion tailwind. We keep banks as core but reduce 12m return targets by ~3–5% vs Apr 1. NII support from higher-for-longer partially compensates.
  • Rand Hedge Requirement: ZAR down ~6% from recent highs. We now require at least 30–40% of barbell to be in rand-hedge names (gold, Naspers, MTN, DRD) or exporters. This is a structural change from Apr 1.
  • Catalyst threshold tightened: Given regime volatility, we now require catalyst within 3–6m (not 12m) for speculative positions. Extended-horizon plays must have strong quality scores to justify holding through volatility.
  • All other filters unchanged: Value (P/E, P/B vs peers), Quality (ROE, ROIC, debt), Momentum (3–6m trend, volume), Liquidity (>0.5m avg vol), Insider/institutional signals.

Scoring update: Gold miners score highest on Catalyst (10/10) and Value (8/10) given current gold price. Sasol scores high on Momentum (9/10) but carries elevated Risk. Banks score high on Quality (9/10) but lose Catalyst points with fewer cuts ahead.
